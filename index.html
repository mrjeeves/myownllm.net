Free and open source · Works on Mac, Windows, Linux & Raspberry Pi

All your devices, your AI

Devices that share a Network ID find each other on a network you control, share the inference, and pass the conversation between them. No broker, no account, no API key.

Every device makes it stronger

A 4090 in the den answers questions you asked from the Pi in the kitchen. A laptop's mic, a workstation's brain. Each thing you plug in is a +1.

It's its own IT

Ask the AI to add a device, switch networks, approve a peer, read a log, run a script. It does, with your permission for anything that touches your shell or files. You don't manage a fleet, you have a conversation.

What you can actually do with it

Cross-device chat. On-device transcription. An AI that's also your IT.

Ask anywhere. Answer from anywhere.

Drafts, regex, the thing you'd rather not paste into ChatGPT. Start a chat on your phone; finish it at the desk; the gaming PC in the den does the thinking.

Tell it to fix itself

"Add my new Pi." "Move this chat to the workstation." Just ask. The AI walks you through it and runs the commands, asking permission before anything touches your shell or files.

Transcribe meetings, locally

Hit record. Real-time transcript with speakers labelled. The labels stay stable for the whole meeting, not just a window. Never uploaded.

Live notes while you talk

Talking Points keeps a tidy bullet summary growing alongside the transcript. Walk away with notes that already make sense.

For developers

OpenAI-compatible. Agent-capable. Mesh-routed.

127.0.0.1:1473 speaks OpenAI, Ollama, and Anthropic. The chat agent ships with tools for your mesh, filesystem, and shell, all behind per-device permission gates, persisted across reloads. Tools execute on the caller's box even when inference is on a remote peer, so your Pi can borrow the workstation's model and still configure the Pi. Cursor, Continue, Aider, Cline, Zed, Open WebUI all just work.

Developer docs → GitHub 

# point any OpenAI client at your machine
curl http://127.0.0.1:1473/v1/chat/completions \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer myownllm" \
  -d '{
    "model": "myownllm",
    "messages": [{"role":"user","content":"hi"}]
  }'
